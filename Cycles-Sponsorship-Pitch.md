## A Message from Talia: Reclaiming the Narrative

The concept for **Cycles** has been in development my whole life. For years I was caught in repeating patterns of depression, rage, and false confidence. Understanding these as symptoms of PMDD and ADHD finally gave me the language to share my story—and I've learned that experience resonates widely. PMDD alone affects an estimated 31 million people worldwide, a number likely much higher due to frequent misdiagnosis. Whether it's neurodivergence, addiction, or self-sabotage, many of us feel trapped in cycles we can't control.

We live in a world designed to make you feel like you're the problem. An entire industry is built on convincing you that you're "broken" so it can sell you the "answer." That individualized isolation strips us of our humanity. My goal with Cycles is to show my audience they aren't alone and that they don't need to look outside themselves for a cure. I want to provide real value by helping listeners realize the answers are already within them—and that the way out is to connect with yourself and move toward a life that truly supports who you are.

This project is also about reclaiming my own agency as an artist and producer. Like many women in music, I've experienced being dismissed or creatively overridden by men. Only 6% of charting music producers are women. By self-producing this project under the mentorship of Kultar Chohan, I'm taking the wheel back. Beyond my own music, I'm using these skills to provide a safe, accessible production space for underrepresented creators who have felt sidelined, so our voices and our stories are the ones being told.

## The Vision: The Alien's Journey

**Cycles** is a four-part music and visual series exploring the lived experience of PMDD and ADHD. The narrative follows **The Alien** (Duvet) as she navigates the disorientation of the human experience. Rather than a standard release, the project is an immersive loop: the end of the final track bleeds back into the start of the first, mirroring the reality of the cycles we get stuck in.

### The Narrative Arc

Each phase has a distinct sonic and visual identity:

| Phase | Track | Sonic Identity | Visual Concept |
|-------|-------|----------------|----------------|
| **I. Isolation** | Alien's Lullaby (June 2026) | Gentle piano ballad | The Alien alone in a vast field, carrying the weight of isolation and depression in an unfamiliar world. |
| **II. Loss of Agency** | My Own Car (July 2026) | Percussion-driven synth | Daydreaming of life back home, the Alien is haunted then trapped in a sentient self-driving car (2D animation)—a loss of agency and control. |
| **III. Denial** | Fool Me (August 2026) | Confrontational pop/hip-hop | The Alien is "sucked into" and trapped within a human body. She copes through delusional confidence and performance as the cracks begin to show. |
| **IV. Release** | Float (Sept 2026) | Alt-pop / stacked vocals | A moment of brief optimism and freedom as the human body and Alien mind find temporary harmony. |

The cycle closes when the human performance breaks down and the Alien returns to the isolation of the field in *Alien's Lullaby*—highlighting that these cycles repeat rather than resolve neatly.
